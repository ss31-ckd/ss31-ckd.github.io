SS-31 (also known as Elamipretide) is an experimental peptide designed to target mitochondria — the energy-producing structures inside our cells.

Developed as part of a new class of therapeutics called mitochondria-targeted peptides, SS-31 represents a novel approach to addressing cellular energy deficits at their source rather than merely managing symptoms.

How it works

SS-31 binds to cardiolipin, a specialised lipid found in the inner mitochondrial membrane. Researchers believe this interaction may:

  • Improve mitochondrial energy production (ATP)
  • Reduce oxidative stress inside cells
  • Protect cells from damage
  • Enhance cellular function in tissues with high energy demands (heart, brain, skeletal muscle)

Unlike many compounds that act as broad antioxidants, SS-31 is selective — it concentrates precisely where mitochondrial damage occurs, potentially offering a more targeted therapeutic effect.

What has it been studied for?

SS-31 has been investigated in clinical and preclinical research for a range of conditions, including:

  • Heart failure and cardiac dysfunction
  • Mitochondrial diseases
  • Kidney disease (including CKD)
  • Muscle fatigue and exercise intolerance
  • Age-related eye diseases

Is it FDA approved?

✅ Yes – as of September 19, 2025, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration granted accelerated approval to elamipretide, marketed as Forzinity™, for the treatment of Barth syndrome in adults and children weighing at least 30 kg.

This marks the first FDA-approved treatment for Barth syndrome and the first therapy directly targeting mitochondrial cardiolipin.

Why are people interested in it?

SS-31 has gained attention in longevity and biohacking communities because mitochondrial dysfunction is thought to play a central role in aging and many chronic diseases, including chronic kidney disease (CKD).

However, it's important to note that many claims made online go well beyond what has been proven in rigorous human studies. Always interpret with caution and rely on peer-reviewed research.
